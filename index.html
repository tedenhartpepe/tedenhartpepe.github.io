Ellicott City Disasters: Applying NASA Resources to a Statistical Flood Model to Improve Early Warning Systems and Public Safety with NASA|DEVELOP
This machine learning study represents the first step in improving early warning system capabilities using open data and NASA Earth observations. The resultant flood model (“Flood Learning Model Environment” or FLuME) evaluated
the statistical significance of features engineered from NASA resources such as North American Land Data Assimilation System (NLDAS) and Soil Moisture Active Passive (SMAP), and utilized stream gauge data from the Department of Homeland Security, Office of Emergency Management, and the United States Geological Survey.

Predicting Home Prices

Sale price prediction is a critical area of data science. It is important for buyers, industry professionals, and government. Accurate predictive models are beneficial for buyers and sellers. When both parties have access to the same data, then the best deal for both parties can be negotiated fairly. Price forecasting supports municipalities as a way to levy consistent and fair property tax rates.

Environmental Flood Modeling

This flood model was built using training data in Calgary and, then, applied to the city of Quebec. I was interested in this project because remote sensing was used to complete landcover data sets and hydrology is a particular domain of interest.

Predicting Water Service Failure

This study evaluates the capabilities of four different modeling methods, inverse distance weighted interpolation, ordinary Kriging interpolation, cartographic modeling, and machine learning, to forecast Puerto Rico Aqueducts and Sewers Authority’s drinking water service vulnerability to failure in the next storm event.

Mapping Yap’s Culture of Cultivation: Research Review & Pilot Study
of Subsistence Garden Networks on the High Islands of Yap, Micronesia

This study is interesting because it employs remote sensing and image classification to generate new data. ArcGIS is used to model geographic space and identification of suitable agriculture sites is automated. This project was presented at the International Small Islands Studies Association in 2018.

Urban Growth Modeling

This project focuses on sprawl in Greater San Antonio. A logistic regression is used to predict demand and supply or development, enabling planners to direct new development to suitable areas. I find this project interesting because the method presented here can be applied to diverse contexts in different cities. Modeling sprawl informs smart growth and can impact a multitude of other aspects of urban life, such as public expenditure, supply, demand, land use, traffic volume, public welfare, and environmental problems and protection.
